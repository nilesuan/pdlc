# 01-discover-exit.md — Phase 01 (Discover) exit checklist

**Authoritative source:** [`../../../handbook/01-discover.md`](../../../handbook/01-discover.md).

This checklist is run at the end of `/discover`. A finding is raised for any unchecked item that should have been completed.

## Done-when

- [ ] **Problem statement written** in `[Segment] needs [job], because [insight], but [obstacle]` form.
- [ ] **≥ 5 customer / user interviews completed** (target ≥ 10 for non-trivial scope), notes captured.
- [ ] **Story-based interview prompts used** (concrete past behavior), not opinion-based prompts ("would you use…?").
- [ ] **Top 3 desired outcomes** identified with importance + satisfaction scores (Outcome-Driven Innovation framing).
- [ ] **JTBD job statement** written for the primary segment (Christensen).
- [ ] **Four big risks named and rated** (desirability, viability, feasibility, usability — Cagan).
- [ ] **Competitor / alternative landscape sketched** — what users do today (including spreadsheets, manual processes, "do nothing").
- [ ] **Hypotheses listed with falsifiable tests** — at least one per identified risk.
- [ ] **Decision artifact** written: pursue / kill / refine, with rationale.
- [ ] **Open questions / unknowns documented** in the discovery doc.

## Auto-rejection

| Trigger | Severity |
|---|---|
| Decision to pursue with < 5 user interviews | Major |
| No falsifiable hypothesis tied to a stated risk | Major |
| Solution-leading prompts in interview notes ("we asked: would you use a feature that…") | Major |
| Problem statement missing segment, job, or obstacle | Major |
| Competitor analysis absent | Minor |
| No "kill criteria" defined | Minor |

## What good looks like

- Quotes from real users in the discovery doc, not paraphrased composites.
- A "what we're NOT building" line that's at least as specific as the "what we are building" line.
- Risks ranked by likelihood × impact, not just listed.
- A clear next step (build a spike? more interviews? kill?), not "we'll see".
